If you're looking to safely 3D-print accessories for use inside a filament dryer (running up to 65°C) using a Bambu Lab H2D, PETG-CF and PCTG are your best bets: both offer heat-deflection temperatures (HDT) exceeding those of standard PLA and PETG, print easily, and are indoor-safe. For higher thermal margins or load-bearing parts, Polycarbonate (PC, PC-FR) is a strong alternative, though it requires a slightly more demanding workflow. PAHT-CF (carbon fiber nylon) provides exceptional thermal and mechanical performance but is overkill unless you need its unique stiffness and strength, with printing challenges to match. For most dryer-accessory needs, PETG-CF combines simplicity, compatibility, and safety—making it the practical go-to.

Key Materials & Findings:
- **PETG-CF:** Easy to print, HDT ~80–85°C, indoor-safe; slight upgrade over PETG.
- **PCTG:** Minor improvement over PETG, similar printability and safety.
- **Polycarbonate/PC-FR:** HDT ~115–135°C, greatly expands thermal margin, moderate indoor safety.
- **PAHT-CF:** HDT >120°C, best mechanical properties, demanding workflow.
